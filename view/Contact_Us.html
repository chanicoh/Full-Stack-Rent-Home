Help Center Frequently Asked Questions

Top FAQs for renters

  • Before committing to a rental, ensure you fully understand the lease terms and conditions. Ask about the duration of the lease, rent amount, security deposit, pet policies, and any other rules or restrictions. Be clear on what utilities are included and which ones you are responsible for.

  • The application process may vary, but generally, you'll need to fill out an application form provided by the landlord or property management. This form may include personal and financial information, rental history, and references. Be prepared to provide proof of income and undergo a background check.

  • While I can provide information and tips on the rental process, I'm not a dedicated apartment-finding service. Consider using real estate websites, apps, or local real estate agents to help you find suitable rental properties in your desired location.

  • To avoid rental scams, be cautious of deals that seem too good to be true, always visit the property before making any payments, and be wary of landlords asking for wire transfers or cash. Use reputable rental platforms, and never provide personal or financial information unless you are certain of the legitimacy of the listing.

  • If a property manager is not responding, try reaching out through multiple channels such as phone, email, and any other provided contact information. If all else fails, consider contacting the property owner directly if possible. Document all your attempts to communicate. If the issue persists, you may need to explore legal options or seek advice from a tenant's rights organization depending on your location.
